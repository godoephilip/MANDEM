Power is the rate of doing work. In terms of electricity, power may be defined as the rate at which electrical energy is generated or consumed. The SI unit for power is Watt (W). The power rating (wattage) of an electrical appliance is the amount of electrical energy it can consume in one second (e.g. A 60Wincandescent bulb consumes 60 joules of electrical energy every one second). On the other hand, the power generator is the amount of electrical energy it can generate per second (e.g. An 80kW generator generates 80kilo joules of electrical energy every one second).
